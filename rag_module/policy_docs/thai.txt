[Date Format]  
In Thai, dates are generally written in the order "day + month + year."  
Example: 22 ตุลาคม 2023

[Time Format]  
Both 12-hour and 24-hour time formats are used in Thailand.  
Hours and minutes are typically separated using a **dot (`.`)**.  
Examples: 14.30, 09.00

[Number Format]  
Thousands are separated by a **comma (`,`)**.  
Examples: 1,000 / 1,000,000

Decimal points are written using a **dot (`.`)**.  
Example: 3.14

[Currency Format]  
Currency symbols are placed **before** the number, usually without a space.  
Examples: ฿1,200, $950, €1,000

Distinguish between currency units (e.g., won, 円) and currency symbols (e.g., ₩, ¥, $), and evaluate only the currency symbols.
