[Date Format]  
In Polish, dates are generally written in the order of "weekday + day + month + year."  
Example: poniedziałek 22 października 2023  
Month names change according to grammatical case, most commonly using the genitive form.  
Example: październik → października

[Time Format]  
Polish typically uses the 24-hour clock system.  
Examples: 14:30, 09:00

[Number Format]  
Thousands are separated using a **space**.  
Examples: 1 000, 1 000 000  
Informally, a period (`.`) may also be used as a separator.

Decimal points are written using a **comma (`,`)**.  
Example: 3,14

[Currency Format]  
The Polish złoty (zł) is placed **after** the number, without a space.  
Example: 1 200zł

Currency symbols other than zł (such as the euro (€) and dollar ($)) are placed **before** the number, typically without a space.  
Examples: €1 200, $950

Distinguish between currency units (e.g., won, 円) and currency symbols (e.g., ₩, ¥, $), and evaluate only the currency symbols.
