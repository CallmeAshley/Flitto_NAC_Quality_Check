[Date Format]  
In Russian, dates are generally written in the order of "weekday + day + month + year."  
Examples: 22 марта 2023, среда 22 марта 2023  
Including the weekday is optional.  
Month names are typically written in the genitive case.  
Examples: март → марта, июнь → июня

[Time Format]  
Russian typically uses the 24-hour clock system.  
Examples: 14:30, 09:00

[Number Format]  
Thousands are separated by a space.  
Examples: 1 000, 1 000 000

Decimal points are written using a comma (`,`) instead of a dot (`.`).  
Example: 3,14

[Currency Format]  
The Russian ruble (₽) is placed directly after the number without a space.  
Example: 1 200₽

Currency symbols other than the ruble (₽) are placed before the number, usually without a space.  
Examples: €1 200, $950

Distinguish between currency units (e.g., won, 円) and currency symbols (e.g., ₩, ¥, $), and evaluate only the currency symbols.
