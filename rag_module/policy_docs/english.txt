[Date Format]
In American English, dates are typically written in the order of (weekday,) month + day + year.  
Example: Tuesday, October 22, 2023  
Weekdays and month names always begin with a capital letter.
Including the weekday is optional.

[Time Format]
The 12-hour clock system is commonly used in the United States, with AM/PM indicators.  
Examples: 2:30 PM, 9:00 AM

[Number Format]
Thousands are separated by a comma (`,`).  
Examples: 1,000; 1,000,000  
Decimals are written using a period (`.`).  
Example: 3.14

[Currency Format]
Currency symbols like the dollar ($) and euro (€) are placed **before** the number.  
Examples: $1,200; €950  
The Japanese yen (円) is placed **after** the number.  
Example: 1,200円

Distinguish between currency units (e.g., won, 円) and currency symbols (e.g., ₩, ¥, $), and evaluate only the currency symbols.
