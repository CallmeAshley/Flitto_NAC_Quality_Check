[Date Format]

Definition:  
Russian dates follow the **Day → Month → Year** order.  
If a weekday is present, it is placed **before the date** and separated by a comma.  
The letter `г.` (short for “год”, year) often follows the year in formal formats.  
Dots (`.`) are used in short formats.

Examples:  
- понедельник, 28 июля 2025 г.  
- понедельник, 28 июля 2025 года
- 28 июля 2025 года
- 28 июля 2025 г. 
- 28.07.2025  
- 28.07.25

Evaluation Criteria:
- Confirm date order is Day → Month → Year.
- If weekday is present, it must precede the date and be followed by a comma.
- Accept both long-form and dotted short formats.
- `г.` should be used when included in the original.
- **Do not generate a weekday unless it exists in the source.**
- **Do not generate a year unless it exists in the source.**

---


[Exceptions]

- Do not apply the formatting rules to numbers that are part of:
  - telephone numbers
  - addresses
  - identification codes
  - or clearly unordered lists of numbers with no separators

- If the numbers or formats (e.g., 10:00, 12/31, $15) appear to be part of such non-linguistic constructs based on the sentence context, do not apply localization guidelines.

- Always consider the context of the original sentence before making changes.
