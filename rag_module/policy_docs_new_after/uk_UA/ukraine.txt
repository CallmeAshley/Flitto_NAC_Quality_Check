[Locale: uk-UA]

[Numeric Format]

Definition:  
Ukrainian uses continental European-style formatting:
- A **comma (`,`)** is used for decimal points.
- A **space** is used every three digits for grouping in the integer part.
- No grouping is used after the decimal.

Examples:  
- 1 234,56  
- 123 456 789,00

Evaluation Criteria:
- Use a space as the thousands separator.
- Use a comma (`,`) for the decimal separator.
- Do not apply grouping after the decimal point.
- Do not use dots or inconsistent spacing.

[Currency Format]

Shared Principle for Currency Symbols and Codes:  
LLMs must handle a wide variety of currency symbols and ISO codes in translations.  
Even if the currency is not Ukrainian hryvnia (₴), the original representation format — **symbol**, **code**, or **name** — must be preserved in the translation.  
Only **position and spacing** should be adapted according to Ukrainian conventions.

Expected currency symbols include:  
₴, $, ¢, €, ¥, £, ₩, ₹, ₽, ₺, ฿, ₱, ₡, ₨, ₦, ₫, ₭, ₲, ₵, ₿, ¤, ƒ, ₮, ₪, ₴, ﷼, ₸, ₾

Expected ISO currency codes include:  
UAH, USD, EUR, JPY, GBP, KRW, CNY, INR, RUB, TRY, AUD, CAD, CHF, MXN, BRL, PLN, SEK, NOK, DKK, CZK, HUF, ILS, SAR, AED, SGD, HKD, TWD, THB, MYR, IDR, PHP, VND, ZAR, NZD

Important:  
Do not convert between currency formats.  
If the source uses a **symbol** (e.g., `$400`), preserve the **symbol** in the translation.  
Do not rewrite it as a **name** (`400 доларів`) or **code** (`USD 400`).  
The same applies in reverse. Always preserve the representation type from the source.

---

1. Currency Symbol Format

Definition:  
Currency symbols such as `₴`, `$`, `€` appear **after the number**, with a **space**.

Examples:  
- 12 345,00 ₴  
- 10 000,00 €  
- 850,00 $

Evaluation Criteria:
- Symbol must follow the number.
- Use a space between number and symbol.
- Follow [Numeric Format] rules for the number.

---

2. Currency Code Format

Definition:  
Currency codes (e.g., UAH, USD) appear **after the number**, separated by a space.

Examples:  
- 12 345,00 UAH  
- 850,00 USD  
- 10 000,00 EUR

Evaluation Criteria:
- Use a space between number and code.
- Use uppercase ISO codes.
- Follow [Numeric Format] rules.

---

3. Currency Name Format

Definition:  
Currency names such as “гривень”, “доларів”, or “єн” appear **after the number**, with a space.  
Ukrainian uses grammatical declension depending on quantity (e.g., долар → доларів).

Examples:  
- 1 000 гривень  
- 850 доларів  
- 10 000 єн  
- 300 євро  
- 1 200 вонів

Evaluation Criteria:
- Name must appear after the number with a space.
- Use correct declension based on quantity.
- Do not generate currency names unless present in the source.
- Follow [Numeric Format] rules.

[Date Format]

Definition:  
Ukrainian dates follow the **Day → Month → Year** order.  
If a weekday is present, it must appear **before** the date and be followed by a comma.  
The abbreviation “р.” (for “рік”) follows the year.

Examples:  
- понеділок, 28 липня 2025 р.  
- 28 липня 2025 р.  
- 28.07.2025  
- 28.07.25

Evaluation Criteria:
- Confirm Day → Month → Year order.
- If weekday is present, place before the date with comma.
- Do not generate weekday if absent in the source.
- Include “р.” if it appears in the original.
- Accept both full month and short numerical formats.

---

[Time Format]

Definition:  
Ukrainian uses the **24-hour clock**, separated by colons (`:`).  
Time zones (e.g., EET) appear only when explicitly present.

Examples:  
- 14:30  
- 14:30:45

Evaluation Criteria:
- Use colons to separate time units.
- Do not use AM/PM.
- Always use two digits (e.g., 09:00 not 9:0).
- Include time zones only if in the source.
